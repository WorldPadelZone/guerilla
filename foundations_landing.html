Prompt Engineering: Thinking, Not Syntax

Forget "prompt hacks." Master the framework instead.

The 4-Part Prompt Structure

1

ROLE

"Act as a creative director at a bold, anti-traditional South African agency..."

2

CONTEXT

"Our client is Toyota. Target audience: young urban professionals (25-35) in Joburg and Cape Town. Brand tone: confident, adventurous, not corporate."

3

TASK

"Generate 10 billboard headlines for the new Urban Cruiser SUV."

4

CONSTRAINTS

"Max 7 words per headline. Avoid clich√©s like 'your journey' or 'discover.' Focus on urban dominance and freedom."

Real Guerilla Prompt Example

"Act as a creative technologist specializing in AR experiences. Our client sells premium vaping products. Generate 20 innovative ways to use AR to enhance the customer experience. Focus on mobile-first, easy-to-use interactions that feel magical but not gimmicky. Target: South African market."

Result: 20 concept seeds in 2 minutes ‚Üí Team selects top 8 ‚Üí Midjourney visualizes ‚Üí Client presentation ready in 4 hours

When AI Outputs Are Bad

‚ùå Don't: Blame the AI or give up

‚úÖ Do: Refine your prompt. Add specificity. Provide examples. Iterate.

üåü Golden Rule: If AI gives you generic output, you gave it a generic prompt. Specificity = Quality.
